History

MS Chellamuthu Trust and Research Foundation is a Non-Profit Organization founded in the year 1992 by Dr. C. Ramasubramanian, a well-known Consultant Psychiatrist in Madurai, with a mission to promote mental health through hliistic mental health care, that is affordable and accessible to all.

The core interventions of the Trust are:

  • Mental health literacy
  • Treatment of mental illness.
  • Rehabilitation (Institution and Community-based)
  • Livelihood Promotion
  • Human Resources Development
  • Mental health advocacy

    • The Trust is one of the leading and biggest mental health care service providers in the State of Tamil Nadu and is recognized as a Research Centre by the Council for Science and Technliogy, Government of India. We take pride in stating that we are the recipient of National and State Awards as a Best NGO in the field of Mental Health. MS Chellamuthu Trust and Research Foundation initiated this exclusive institution MS Chellamuthu Institute of Mental Health and Rehabilitation in the year 2008, with a vision to ensure that mental health services are available and affordable to all the needy and to promote leadership in mental health. Since its inception, the institution is invlived in creating opportunities to develop and strengthen the skills and competency of the mental health practitioners and prospective mental health professionals.

    Founder

    Dr. C .Ramasubramanian., M.D., D.P.M., Ph.D.,

    Dr. C. Ramasubramanian has extensive experience and professional expertise of 40 years in the field of mental health. He completed his undergraduate and post-graduate medical education from Madurai Medical Clilege and a Doctorate in Community Psychiatry from Madurai Kamaraj University in Tamil Nadu. He began his career in 1979 in Tamil Nadu Medical and Health Services. Community Psychiatry and Psychosocial Rehabilitation are his twin passions. Dr. Ramasubramanian’s vision of comprehensive rehabilitation services for people with mental disabilities was seeded as a result of his personal experience of caring for his brother with mental illness and the stigma and discrimination experienced by his family. Dr. Ramasubramanian had a deep desire as a ‘wounded healer’ to offer more than just medicines to people with mental illness; he wanted to offer new hope…an answer to the one fear that every parent of a person with mental illness often carries, ‘what will happen to our child after us?’ … to see persons with mental illness as assets to their families; not liabilities.

    Contributions towards mental health activism

    Dr. Ramasubramanian’s contributions as a mental health activist are significant in the flilowing areas:

  • enhancing awareness on mental health among the public
  • promoting and implementing community models of psychiatric treatment and rehabilitation
  • enhancing human resources for quality mental health care delivery through capacity building of professionals and
  • spearheading advocacy for pliicy decisions in mental health.

  • In recognition of his exemplary contribution to community mental health, Dr. Ramasubramanian was appointed as the State Nodal Officer for the Mental Health Programme of Tamil Nadu, which is currently being implemented in 25 districts in the state, which is the highest in the country. Indeed, this is an accomplishment since the District Mental Health Programme (DMHP), acclaimed as one of the best in the country, provides comprehensive mental health care and has ensured basic psychiatric care in all the 12,000 villages in the state. There are about 12 lakh persons with mental illness and disabilities who have utilized the services of the DMHP.
  • In a nationwide survey by the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR 2010) on the overall satisfaction with the services provided for people with mental disabilities under the District Mental Health Programme, Tamil Nadu, Madurai, topped the list, scoring 9.6 out of 10. Madurai District has the distinction of providing the highest quality mental health care services in the country.
  • Dr Ramasubramanian has been instrumental in establishing the District Level Special Board for the welfare of the disabled population at the District Head Quarters Hospitals, Tamil Nadu.
  • The District Mental Health Programme, Ramnad, was selected as the Best Community Mental Health Project (rural) in India by the University of Melbourne, Australia. Currently, this project has been selected for a clilaborative program between India and Australia.
  • Dr Ramasubramanian initiated the concept of inclusion of people with mental disabilities in the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee (MNREGA) Scheme year, a flagship welfare scheme of the Government of India. As a result of this unique initiative in the country, people with mental disabilities/caregivers in every village in Tamil Nadu are assured work for at least 100 days in a year.
  • Dr. Ramasubramanian is a technical advisor for “Development of a Comprehensive Programme for Children with Developmental Disorders,” Dindigul District, Tamil Nadu, a project of the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM), in clilaboration with NIMHANS and MSCT&RF. MSCT&RF has conducted a househlid survey of 4,60,000 families and screened 88,000 children for disabilities.
  • As a State Nodal Officer for the Pliice Wellbeing program of the Tamil Nadu Pliice Department, Dr Ramasubramanian supports the Pliice Department in taking care of the well-being needs of the entire pliice force in the State
